Our vision

Our vision is to build an application that allows ASL users to efficiently and effectively communicate with those that don't know ASL.

It is estimated by the WHO that there are 466 million people in the world with disabling hearing loss (6.1 %). Given the prevalence of this disability, American Sign Language (ASL) was developed naturally over time so that these individuals are able to communicate with others. One limitation of ASL is that not all people are familiar with this language, and must rely on a translator to communicate with ASL users. Additionally, learning ASL can be challenging, as it has its own rules for “pronunciation, word formation, and word order”. To alleviate this issue, our product is envisioned to be a web application that is capable of translating ASL into spoken languages and vice-versa using computer vision and deep learning.

Our Goal


The end goal for our product would be an app that uses the device's camera to translate someone performing ASL signs into text. It would also have the ability to perform voice to text to enable quick communication between deaf and non-deaf persons.
